Gluteoplasty

The gluteal prosthesis is indicated for patients with small buttocks or who, after weight loss, had a great reduction in volume, without significant ptosis (slump of the buttocks).

Surgery
The surgery is performed in the hospital to prevent infection, under epidural anesthesia, and lasts about 1 hour and a half. Hospital discharge usually occurs after 24 hours. It is performed by placing a silicone gel prosthesis inside the gluteus major muscle. The scar is hidden in the gluteal groove and is difficult to perceive.

Types of Core Prostheses
There are several types and models of gluteal prostheses, each with an indication depending on the type of buttocks of the patient. Gluteal prostheses can vary in profile and wrap. The profile can be round or anatomical, depending on the projection of the prosthesis. The content is high cohesive silicone gel. The denture wrap can also vary, being textured or smooth. The choice of the ideal gluteal prosthesis will be made at the medical consultation, when the surgeon will examine the patient and choose the best prosthesis to produce the best result for the augmentation of the gluteal region, which in each patient is different.

Postoperative
The postoperative period of augmentation gluteoplasty with prosthesis is usually a little painful in the first 48 hours, when analgesics and anti-inflammatory drugs are used to alleviate the discomfort. You should avoid lying on your back for a month.
The result in terms of shape is already quite evident in the postoperative period. During the first two months there is a reduction of edema and healing gains strength. The definitive result of the augmentation gluteoplasty occurs after 6 months, due to the maturation of the scar.

Important Issues

DOES AUGMENTATION GLUTEOPLASTY LEAVE SCARS?

Every surgery leaves a scar. Fortunately, this surgery allows us to place the scar in a hidden place. The scar is usually small and of good quality, which added to a hidden location makes it quite discreet. The scar size will be as small as possible to place the gluteal prosthesis, usually 7 cm.

WHERE IS THE SCAR LOCATED?

The scar is located in the intergluteal groove, a natural fold between the buttocks. The size of the scar is around 7 cm, the smallest size that allows the placement of the gluteal prosthesis.

I HEAR THAT SOME PATIENTS GET VERY VISIBLE SCARS.

The intergluteal crease is not a place that usually gives poor quality scars, and the scars are usually very discreet.

HOW WILL MY BUTTOCKS LOOK IN RELATION TO SIZE AND CONSISTENCY AFTER PLACING THE PROSTHESIS?

The buttocks can have their volume increased through gluteal prosthesis surgery. In addition, we aim to improve its consistency and shape with surgical intervention. So, in this case, you can choose the new volume, as we have several sizes of silicone pieces to be introduced. There is a harmony between the ideal volume of the buttocks and the size of the trunk, a characteristic that must be preserved in the planning of the surgery. The gluteal implant has a greater consistency than the normal tissue of the buttocks, so a certain degree of increase in the consistency of the buttocks after placement of the implants is common.

CAN I CHOOSE THE SHAPE OF THE PROSTHESIS I WANT?

Each patient differs in relation to the volume, consistency and shape of the buttocks. Therefore, it is not because a friend or model put on a certain type and volume of gluteal prosthesis that you will put on the same. Each patient has an ideal gluteal prosthesis, which will be chosen in consultation after a thorough examination of the region and the patient's biotype.

WHEN WILL I ACHIEVE THE DEFINITIVE RESULT OF INCREASE GLUTEOPLASTY WITH PROSTHESIS?

Although the immediate result is very good, it is only in the late period that the buttocks will reach their definitive shape (after 6 months). Most of the edema (swelling) disappears after 2 months of implant placement, which is the period of greatest changes in the shape of the buttocks.

IS THE POSTOPERATIVE OF THIS SURGERY PAINFUL?

It is usually a little painful for the first few days, due to the muscle strain. The pain will be controlled with analgesics and anti-inflammatories and will subside over time.

WHAT KIND OF ANESTHESIA IS USED?

Epidural anesthesia.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGICAL ACT TAKE?

On average 90 minutes.

WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL PERIOD?

24 hours.

WHEN ARE THE POINTS REMOVED?

They are usually removed after 15 to 21 days.

ARE DRAINS USED?

Drains are not used.

WHEN CAN I TAKE A FULL BATH?

The day after surgery.

WHEN CAN I RETURN TO MY GYMNASTICS?

Usually after 60 days, with progressive exercises.

CAN THE BUTTLE PROSTHESIS SURGERY BE ASSOCIATED WITH OTHER PLASTIC SURGERIES?

Yes, the association with liposuction is very common.
