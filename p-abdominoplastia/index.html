Tummy tuck (Abdominoplasty)

After pregnancy or a violent diet, it is extremely common for women to have a bulging abdomen, due to sagging skin and muscles. Abdominoplasty is the tummy tuck used to remove excess skin in the region, correct muscle flaccidity, and often associated with Liposuction, improve body contour.

Surgery
Abdominoplasty or dermolipectomy is always performed in the hospital under epidural anesthesia combined with sedation, which makes you sleepy during the procedure. Discharge is given 24 or 48 hours after surgery.

Postoperative
You can walk in the hospital on the same day to avoid thrombosis, but be slightly bent over to protect the scars.
There is usually no pain in the postoperative period. Efforts, driving cars and carrying weights for a month should be avoided. Physical exercises are allowed after 2 months, and until then you must use a modeling belt.
The return to the clinic will be made after 7 days. The stitches will be removed until the 15th day. The final result you will see after 6 months.

Important Issues

WHICH IS?

It is the surgery performed on the abdomen in order to correct excess skin, fat and sagging muscles.

WHAT ARE THE MAIN CAUSES OF ABDOMINAL Sagging?

Lack of physical exercise, sedentary lifestyle, hereditary factors, multiple pregnancies or large weight variation.

CAN MEN DO IT?

Yes, men can also benefit from this treatment.

WHICH ANESTHESIA IS USED?

Epidural or general.

IS HOSPITALIZATION NECESSARY?

Yes, for 24 hours.

WHERE ARE THE SCARS?

here is a small scar around the navel and another horizontal scar near the pubis, which extends laterally. They are usually barely noticeable over time and easily hidden by panties or bikini.

CAN I GET PREGNANT?

Yup. It is important to try not to gain too much weight during pregnancy. This care also applies to pregnant women who have never had an operation.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF IN THE SUN?

As long as the scars are protected with a bathing suit, bikini or paper tape, there is no harm in sunbathing from the second month onwards. The use of sunscreen is recommended.
