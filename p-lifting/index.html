Face Lift

As a person ages, the effect of gravity, sun exposure and daily stress can be seen on the face, resulting in sagging skin and musculature, formation of wrinkles at various points, fat deposition in the jaw and sagging in the neck. Facial Lifting Surgery aims to correct these imperfections, and can be associated with various procedures such as peelings, nose, ear and eyelid plastic surgery, leading to an aspect of facial rejuvenation. A facelift does not have a pre-determined age to be done, nor does it stop the aging process, nor does it leave the person with another physiognomy, but rather seeks to “refresh” and “rejuvenate” the facial aspect.

In 10 years you will have aged 10 years, but you will look better than if you had not had the operation.

Surgery
The surgery is performed in the hospital, being performed under local anesthesia and sedation and the patient remains hospitalized for 24 hours. A full facelift has incisions in the scalp, inside and behind the ears. This way we avoid the incision in front of the ear that is visible in some cases. The complete surgery lasts about 4 hours, being an act of whim and not a race against time. Except in very mild cases, we always replace the muscles, which leads to a much more lasting and natural result. After the surgery, we use a “helmet”, being common to place drains in the first 24 hours.
Postoperative
After the surgery you will be swollen for about 10 days, with purplish areas, but it is rare to feel any pain, and you should keep your head elevated for the first 3 days. The return to the clinic is made after 4 or 5 days when the removal of the stitches begins, the last ones being removed after 12 days. After 24 hours of surgery you will be able to wash your head, using plenty of shampoo and cream and not using a hot dryer for 20 days. Any physical effort should be avoided for 30 days. After the first return to the clinic, facial drainage may be indicated, as well as the use of specific cosmetic products. The scars will be evident for about 3 months and after the stitches are removed, a specific ointment will be prescribed for their disappearance.

Important Issues

WHICH IS?

Facelift or rhytidectomy is surgery performed on the face and neck, with the purpose of attenuating the signs of aging.

WHAT ARE THE SIGNS OF FACE AGING?

The signs of aging are evident through the deep grooves around the mouth, sagging skin, loss of jaw and neck contour, excess fat in the double chin, wrinkles on the forehead, lips and between the eyebrows, crow's feet, excess skin and fat on the eyelids, fall of the lateral portion of the eyebrows and the corner of the mouth and little projection of the lips. These changes can occur together or separately.

WHAT ARE THE CAUSES OF FACE AGING?

There are many factors that contribute to facial aging. Among them are genetic characteristics, stress, sun exposure (photoaging), inadequate diet, excessive consumption of alcohol and cigarettes.

FROM WHAT AGE IS IT POSSIBLE TO OPERATE THE FACE?

There is no ideal age and each case must be evaluated. The sooner the surgery is performed, the better the quality of the skin and, consequently, its elastic and retraction power, thus bringing better results.

WHAT KIND OF ANESTHESIA IS USED? IS HOSPITALIZATION NECESSARY?

Usually the anesthesia used is local. The anesthesiologist will be present in the operating room, leaving the patient sedated and calm with the use of medications. The surgery is outpatient, that is, discharge occurs on the same day as the surgery.

HOW ARE THE CURATIVES?

Dressings are not normally used, thus providing greater comfort and well-being to the patient, in addition to less edema. The hygiene of the face and scalp are also facilitated. The hair can be washed the next day.

WHERE ARE THE INCISIONS LOCATED AND WHEN ARE THE STITCHES REMOVED?

It will depend on each case. In general, they are located inside the scalp, in the region before the ear and behind it, being very little noticeable. In the upper eyelids, they are located in the groove that we form when we open our eyes. In the lower ones, close to the eyelashes or even internally (inside the eye). From the 3rd day, the stitches are removed, when it is possible to use makeup. Depending on the case, one can choose to use video surgery where appropriate video cameras and instruments are available, reducing the size of the scars.

HOW IS THE POST-OPERATIVE AND WHEN IS IT POSSIBLE TO PERCEIVE THE RESULT?

There is no pain or discomfort in the postoperative period, despite some initial swelling. In the first few weeks there is a decrease in the sensitivity of the face that improves in a short time. Return to routine takes approximately one week. The use of sunscreen is mandatory. The result, although it can be appreciated in the first few days, improves over the weeks.

WHAT ARE AND HOW LONG DO THE RESULTS LAST?

The face becomes more rejuvenated, expressive and cheerful, with less marked furrows and better defined contours. The results should always be natural, not letting it appear that the person has had surgery. The duration of results varies from case to case but does not last for life.

IS IT POSSIBLE TO ASSOCIATE MORE THAN ONE FACE SURGERY?

Yes, rhytidectomy is usually associated with eyelid surgery, double chin liposuction, lip dermabrasion, laser or a peeling.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF IN THE SUN?

As long as you use a suitable sunscreen, in a month you can be exposed to the sun.
