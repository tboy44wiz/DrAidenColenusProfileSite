Blepharoplasty (Eyelid Surgery)

The eyes play an important role in the appearance of the face. They communicate our feelings and emotions. The structures that mirror our emotions are found around the eyes: the upper and lower eyelids.
As time does not stop, with age the eyelids become more droopy and aged and the eyes begin to reflect the image of an elderly person, tired and with a dull look. This is when eyelid surgery or blepharoplasty can be very helpful.
Technological advances achieved in recent years in the area of cosmetic eyelid surgery have made it possible to obtain remarkable and very natural results. Improvement can be observed both physically and psychologically.

Eyelids
The first signs of aging are seen around the eyes. The skin of the eyelids becomes thinner, wrinkled and loses its elasticity, thus forming a fold. The muscles around the eyes lose their tone and become thinner. In some people, fatty accumulations appear on the upper and lower eyelids, forming bags, while in others, with a small amount of fat, the eyes tend to be sunken.

Factors that influence eyelid aging
-Excessive sun exposure
-Genetic predisposition
-Decreased muscle tone from repeated pulling of the eyelids when applying makeup
-Health problems such as allergies, heart or thyroid problems

Changes in the eyelids at various stages of life
From childhood to youth, the skin texture is firm, without wrinkles. The orbicularis musculature is firm and the eyelids have good tonicity. There is no bulging of fat or dark circles under the eyes.
Dos From twenty to forty years of age, the first wrinkles appear and “crow's feet” begin. The muscles lose part of their tone, with the appearance of a discreet bulging of the fat. The appearance of dark circles begins. From forty to sixty years of age, excess skin appears on the upper eyelids and wrinkles and crow's feet become more evident. There is an accentuated loss of muscle tone and an increase in fat bags, or the eyes become more sunken.
Dos From the age of sixty onwards there is an excessive increase in skin on the upper eyelids, and the wrinkles and crow's feet become deeper and more abundant. The loss of muscle tone is accentuated and the lower eyelid droops (ectropion). The eyelid bags are more prominent or the eyes are more sunken.

Surgical Intervention Careful
examination of the structures makes it possible to determine the quality of neighboring tissues, as well as the degree of skin aging, leading to an exact diagnosis that makes it possible to choose the most suitable surgical technique for a given case, in order to obtain the best result.
One surgical technique does not fit all cases. There are several options when performing a blepharoplasty, although the incisions made are similar.
Surgical experience, choice of stitches, knowledge, correct judgment of tissue changes along with minute details observed when performing surgery will determine the appearance and final result.

Before and After
The eyelid plastic surgery is performed under local anesthesia associated with sedation and usually lasts 1 hour.

Upper eyelid surgery
The incisions follow the natural folds of the skin, with the advantage of hiding the scars from the surgery. Excess skin and part of the muscle is removed, if necessary. Sometimes it is necessary to remove a part of the fat that causes the eyelids to bulge and make them look tired.

Lower eyelid surgery
The incision is made about 2 mm below the eyelashes, resulting in an imperceptible scar. Excess skin and fat are removed, strengthening the muscles when necessary. In young people, who have protruding bags, excess fat can be removed through the transconjunctival approach (inside the eyelids), without leaving scars. Some people have a drop in the outer corner of the eye that gives an appearance of sadness. This can be corrected by canthopexy, a surgery that repositions and elevates the corner of the eye.

Important Issues

WHICH IS?

Blepharoplasty is eyelid surgery. It aims to treat excess skin and fat bags, in addition to repositioning the outer corner of the eyes.

WHEN IS SURGERY INDICATED? ONLY OLDER PEOPLE SHOULD DO IT?

It is indicated when there is excess skin or fat bags on the eyelids, bringing a tired, sad and expressionless look.

IS THERE AN IDEAL AGE FOR EYELID OPERATIONS?

There is no ideal age, but the ideal opportunity. This opportunity is determined by the presence of the defect to be corrected and can occur at any age.

IS THERE PAIN IN THE POSTOPERATIVE?

Not usually. Even if there is a greater sensitivity or small outbreaks of pain, these can be perfectly abolished with the use of common analgesics.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGERY TAKE?

Usually around 60 minutes.

WHEN WILL I REACH THE DEFINITIVE RESULT?

After the 3rd month. However, right after the 8th day we will already have approximately 25% of the desired result, and in the two or three subsequent weeks these percentages tend to improve markedly.

HOW IS THE POST-OPERATIVE? WHEN ARE THE POINTS REMOVED?

There is no postoperative pain. There may be some tearing, swelling, and ecchymoses (purple spots) that disappear in a few days. The stitches are removed on the 2nd or 3rd day, at which point it is already possible to wear makeup and return to work.

ARE THE EYES VERY SWALLOWED? FOR HOW LONG?

Edema (swelling) of the eyes varies from patient to patient. There are those who, on the 4th or 5th day, look quite natural. Others will achieve this result after the 8th day. Even so, the first 3 days after surgery are the ones in which there is greater “swelling” of the eyelids. The use of sunglasses is useful at this stage, as well as the use of cold compresses that help the regression of edema. Only after the 3rd month can we say that the residual edema is discreet and does not compromise the final result.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGERY TAKE?

Usually around 60 minutes.

WHAT ARE THE “PURPLE BLOTS” OBSERVED IN CERTAIN CASES?

They are nothing more than the infiltration of blood into the underlying skin, and even into the ocular conjunctiva; are due to the surgical trauma itself. This, however, does not constitute any future problem and is not considered a complication, but a transient and reversible intercurrence.

WHEN WILL I REACH THE DEFINITIVE RESULT?

After the 3rd month. However, right after the 8th day we will already have approximately 25% of the desired result, and in the two or three subsequent weeks these percentages tend to improve markedly.

WILL THE EYES BE OCCLUDED AFTER SURGERY?

Not necessarily. We only recommend placing cold compresses for a few minutes, several times a day, an act that is controlled by the patient himself, as a prophylaxis of severe edema.

HOW IS THE POST-OPERATIVE? WHEN ARE THE POINTS REMOVED?

There is no postoperative pain. There may be some tearing, swelling, and ecchymoses (purple spots) that disappear in a few days. The stitches are removed on the 2nd or 3rd day, at which point it is already possible to wear makeup and return to work.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF IN THE SUN? ? 

As long as you use a suitable sunscreen, in two weeks you can be exposed to the sun.

HOW IS THE RESULT AND HOW LONG DOES IT LAST?

The result is natural. The eye region becomes more cheerful and expressive. After a few years, eyelid surgery may be repeated.

WHAT ABOUT THE CHICKEN FEET?

Crow's feet around the eyes do not improve significantly with blepharoplasty and can be treated with Botox and other techniques.

AFTER ALL, DOES THE RESULT PAY?

If you are aware of what you want and the surgeon can provide you with what You asked for, it certainly pays off. However, it is important to take into account the fact that eyelid surgery does not provide general rejuvenation to the face when performed alone.

Many patients expect this result (rejuvenation) with blepharoplasty alone. What should be expected in most cases is a “lighter”, more “rested” aspect of the patient. The plastic surgeon will only improve this territory damaged by pre-existing aesthetic defects.

Facial rejuvenation implies other procedures associated with blepharoplasty. The "crow's feet", even if properly operated, will never disappear completely, leaving the marks, due to the action of the orbicularis muscle and the loss of elasticity of the remaining skin.
