Gynecomastia

Gynecomastia presents as an increase in the volume of the male breast region, usually rounded under the areola. The smaller the presence of fat, the more evident the delimitation of the mammary gland becomes. This presentation is more common in adolescents.

With the increase in the volume of fat in the breasts, the gland becomes less evident, and the breast is diffusely enlarged. This type of clinical presentation is more common in adult and elderly patients.

Surgery
The surgery is performed in the hospital to prevent infection, under local anesthesia with sedation, and takes about 1 hour. Hospital discharge usually occurs after 24 hours.

Postoperative
The patient should be away from efforts for 21 days. A compression garment should be worn for approximately 1 month. It is not common to have significant pain, only mild discomfort, which can be overcome with common painkillers and anti-inflammatories.

Swelling and purple areas in the operated area are common, lasting around 14 to 21 days. There may be a change in the sensitivity of the areola, usually transient.

In the first months, the scar is reddish, generally becoming whitish over time, depending on the patient's genetics. The swelling increased after 21 days, but some remnants may remain for a few months. The redness can exist for 14 to 21 days

The final result of the correction of gynecomastia is reached between 6 months and 1 year, with the maturation of the scar, but after 2 months, 80% of the definitive form is obtained.

Important Issues

DOES SURGERY LEAVE SCARS?

Yes, the scar will depend on the type of breast and the amount of tissue to be removed, but in most cases it is periareolar.

HOW DO THE SCARS LOOK AFTER GYNECOMASTIA SURGERY?

The periareolar scar is usually of good quality, even in patients who have unsightly scars elsewhere.

Certain patients are prone to hypertrophic scarring or keloid scarring. This trend, however, can be predicted, to a certain extent, during the initial consultation, when we will ask you a series of questions about your previous clinical life, as well as family characteristics, which help a lot in terms of the prognosis of scars. Light-skinned people are less likely to suffer from this complication.

HOW LONG WILL IT TAKE TO ACHIEVE THE DEFINITIVE RESULT OF THE GYNECOMASTIA CORRECTION?

Although the immediate result is very good, it is only between the 6th and 12th month that the scar will reach its definitive shape.

IS THE POSTOPERATIVE OF GYNECOMASTIA PAINFUL?

In general, no, as long as you obey the doctor's instructions, especially with regard to arm movement, efforts and other care in the first few days.

IS THERE A DANGER IN THIS OPERATION?

Rarely, gynecomastia surgery has serious complications. This is due to the fact that each patient is properly prepared, in addition to considering the convenience of associating this surgery with others. As the surgery is usually with local anesthesia and sedation, the risk is similar to going to the dentist (also with local anesthesia).

WHAT TYPE OF ANESTHESIA IS USED?

Local anesthesia with sedation is usually used.

HOW LONG DOES THE SURGICAL ACT TAKE?

Depending on each type of gynecomastia, the average is one hour.

WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL PERIOD?

Usually 24h.

WHEN ARE THE POINTS REMOVED FROM A GYNECOMASTIA CORRECTION?

Stitches are usually not removed, as the stitches are internal and absorbed by themselves. When external stitches are used (rarely), they are removed around the seventh to fifteenth day, without major discomfort.

WHEN WILL I TAKE A FULL BATH?

Usually after 1 day.

WHEN CAN I RETURN TO MY GYMNASTICS?

You can gradually return from 21 days, with the return to all sports activities usually after 30 days.

CAN CORRECTION OF GYNECOMASTIA BE ASSOCIATED WITH OTHER PLASTIC SURGERIES?

Yes, it can commonly be associated with liposuction in case there is also an accumulation of fat in the breasts. Other plastic surgeries can also be associated.
