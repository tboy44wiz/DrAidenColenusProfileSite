Rhinoplasty (Nose Plastic)

Nose Plastic Surgery or Rhinoplasty is the surgery used to reshape your nose, reduce or increase its size, soften or raise the tip, and can be performed from 15 years old for girls and 17 years old for boys. In addition, it is used in combination with otorhinolaryngological surgeries to improve respiratory function or with other plastic surgeries such as chin augmentation for better harmony of the facial profile. Surgery Most surgeries are performed in the hospital under general anesthesia, with milder cases being performed in the office under local anesthesia.

The patient leaves the hospital the same day or the next day. The surgery is performed inside the nose, leaving no visible scars, except in cases where the nostrils are reduced or when an âopen rhinoplastyâ is performed for more difficult cases. After the surgery, a plaster is placed over the nose, when there is a need for a bone fracture, or simply a tape is placed over it. The plaster is removed after a week and replaced with tape, and you should avoid wetting the plaster during this time. After 4 days you can return to work, but you should avoid carrying weight, exerting yourself and sunbathing. Except in cases where there is an associated functional surgery, we do not usually use a âplugâ inside the nose. Postoperatively, it is uncommon to feel pain in the area, although you will have swollen and purplish eyelids, which takes about 15 days to get better, and you will have difficulty breathing through your nose for 1 month. Physical exercises are allowed after 2 months. When the cast is removed, your nose will be swollen, and about 70% of this swelling will go away in the first month. The final result you will see between 3 and 6 months after the surgery; when, if necessary, some touch-up will be done in the office.

Important Issues

WHICH IS?

It is the surgery performed on the nose with the aim of improving its shape and obtaining an adequate proportion of this organ with the rest of the structures of the face, in addition to maintaining or improving the quality of breathing. That is why it is called aesthetic-functional rhinoplasty, and is sometimes associated with nasal septum or turbinate surgery.

IS THERE A MINIMUM AGE TO HAVE A RHINOPLASTY?

In general, it is possible to perform a rhinoplasty from the age of 15. Factors such as the degree of development of the nasal structure, the patient's emotional maturity and expectations regarding results should also be taken into account when indicating surgery.

WHERE ARE THE CUTS MADE? ARE THERE POINTS TO BE REMOVED?

In practically all cases, the cuts are made inside the nose, so there are no external scars. The few points that are given from the inside fall off on their own in a few days.

WHAT KIND OF ANESTHESIA IS USED? IS HOSPITALIZATION NECESSARY?

Rhinoplasty is an outpatient surgery where the patient is discharged on the same day. It is usually performed under local anesthesia with the presence of the anesthetist.

DOES RHINOPLASTY LEAVE SCARS?

Not usually. In some cases there are barely visible scars; this is due to the fact that the incisions (cuts) are made âinside the noseâ or in the furrows and consequently do not leave any visible traces. In special cases, when the nasal wings are treated, there are two small scars in the grooves between the wings and the face.

CAN I CHOOSE THE SHAPE I WANT FOR MY FUTURE NOSE?

Not. There is an aesthetic balance between the nose and the face, a balance that the surgeon must observe in order to preserve the naturalness and authenticity of this face. Each case is studied in detail, so that the nose can be given the best possible shape, within the requirements of the face. If your choice coincides with that type of nose planned, your wish will undoubtedly be granted.

IS THE DEFINITIVE RESULT OF THE âNEW SHAPEâ IMMEDIATE?

Not. Several phases are characteristic of the postoperative period of the nose. Thus, in a 1st phase (shortly after the removal of the rigid dressing around the 7th day), despite the correction of several aesthetic defects of the original nose, we noticed an edema (swelling) that decreases with the passing of the days and that tends to completely disappear around the 6th month. There are patients who reach the definitive result a little earlier, as well as others who rarely go beyond this period. HOW WILL MY BREATHING LOOK AFTER SURGERY?

Aesthetic rhinoplasty also aims to improve the patient's respiratory conditions, when these conditions are precarious in the original nose. Over time, it tends to normalize. Respiratory problems are usually linked to the septum which, in many cases, is corrected in the same surgical time.
When the correction requires special care, cosmetic surgery should be performed at a second opportunity, after the septum has been corrected by the specialist.

ARE CURATIVES USED? HOW MANY?

After finishing the surgery, the nose is kept immobilized with plaster or plastic plate, which completely covers it. This dressing remains for about 7 to 8 days, after which it is removed in the office. In some cases we use nasal packing, which is left for 24 to 48 hours. After this period, a micropore dressing is applied directly to the skin to shape it for about 1 week.

I HEAR THAT THE NOSE âBLOODSâ IN THE FIRST DAYS. THIS IS TRUE?

There is a small amount of bleeding through the gauze packing or through the nasal shaper, which is normal in the first 48 hours. This, however, should not be a cause for concern, as a protective dressing, superimposed over the opening of the nose, is purposely kept in order to stop this bleeding. This additional dressing can be changed at home as often as needed.

IS THERE PAIN IN THE POSTOPERATIVE?

Rarely. Aesthetic nose surgery has a painless postoperative period. When any pain occurs, it is easily treated with painkillers, which will be prescribed as a preventive.

HOW IS THE RESULT AND WHEN CAN YOU OBSERVE IT?

The result must always be natural, not showing that surgery was performed. The nose must be in harmony with the rest of the face and its function preserved, that is, the respiratory capacity maintained. It will be a compliment to the surgeon and his patient the comment "it looks like your nose hasn't been operated on". In a few days the nose will be completely different compared to before the surgery and looking good.

The final result takes approximately one year, until the final accommodation of the tissues.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF IN THE SUN?

As long as you use a suitable sunscreen, in a month you can be exposed to the sun.

HOW LONG DOES THE RESULT LAST? IS MORE THAN ONE SURGERY NEEDED?

In general, results last a lifetime.
