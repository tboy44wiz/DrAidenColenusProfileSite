Mammoplasty

Assessment
Careful examination of the shape of the breasts makes it possible to assess the different factors that will determine the choice of the most appropriate surgical technique or procedure. Among them, we can mention the amount of glandular tissue and adipose tissue; number of pregnancies; presence of asymmetry; patient's weight, height and body structure; pectoral muscle volume; patient preference; genetic inheritance; proportion between breasts and other body structures; ethnic group and amount of skin, among others.

RAISING OR REPOSITIONING THE BREASTS
This surgical procedure consists of reducing or correcting sagging of the breasts, caused by sagging skin. It is used by removing excess skin and replacing the breast tissue, “mounting” the breast, using the tissue itself as if it were a prosthesis. Of course, it is not possible to enlarge the breasts with this technique alone. When the patient does not have enough tissue to correct the sagging of the breasts, or intends to increase the breasts, it is necessary to use a silicone prosthesis to perform the surgery.
Mastopexy returns the breasts to the shape and firmness previously presented.

Before and After
The technique to be used depends on the amount of fatty tissue, glandular and skin present, in addition to the size of the areolas and the symmetry of the breasts. Depending on the technique chosen, the resulting scar may be in the shape of an inverted T, a circle around the areola, or a vertical scar below the areola. The scar gradually diminishes and over the course of 1 year it becomes almost imperceptible.
The surgery can be done under general or epidural anesthesia, lasting about 2 hours. Recovery takes about 30 days.

Important Issues

GOOD SIZE BREASTS, BUT FALLEN AND SLAPPY, WHAT TO DO?

In this case, a mastopexy is indicated, which is the surgery performed on the breasts with the intention of correcting their sagging and sagging, without changing the size. This condition usually occurs after significant weight loss or pregnancy. The shape of the breast is improved and the areola is repositioned.

FROM WHAT AGE IS IT INDICATED?

There is no defined age and the indication will depend, in addition to the characteristics of the breasts, on the emotional maturity of the patient.

ARE THERE SCARS?

There will always be scars. Its location will depend on the type of breast and may be restricted only around the areola, in the shape of an “L” or an inverted “T” shape. The final quality of the scars will fundamentally depend on the personal characteristics of each patient and can only be considered definitive after a period of not less than one year. Generally speaking, they become barely noticeable over time.

WHAT TYPE OF ANESTHESIA IS USED?

Epidural or general anesthesia.

IS HOSPITALIZATION NECESSARY?

Yes, for a period of 24 hours.

HOW IS THE POST-OPERATIVE?

The postoperative period is calm and there is usually no pain. There are no large dressings and the patient leaves the hospital with a post-surgical bra. In approximately 15 days, the stitches are removed and the patient can resume her activities. Light physical exercises are allowed in thirty days.

IS IT POSSIBLE TO BREASTFEED AFTER SURGERY? WHAT ABOUT SENSITIVITY?

The ability to breastfeed is maintained and breast sensitivity is preserved after mastopexy.

DO RESULTS LAST FOREVER?

There are several factors that will determine the duration of the result (severity, breast weight, patient weight variation, pregnancy, skin quality, hormonal effects…). Thus, touch-ups or new surgeries may sometimes be necessary within a few years.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF TO THE SUN AND DO GYMNASTIC?

As long as breast scars are protected with a bikini or paper tape, there is no harm in sunbathing after 1 month.

REDUCTION MAMOPLASTY Reduction
mammoplasty aims to reduce the volume of the breasts and/or improve their shape, so that they achieve harmony with the rest of their body contour.
Thus, it is important to remember that different women have different breasts and the postoperative result is individual for each case. Good planning before surgery and discussion with the surgeon about your anxieties and expectations are the best way to obtain a satisfactory result.

PRE-OPERATIVE
Laboratory exams, mammography and/or breast ultrasound and other exams that may be necessary in each case are performed.
Any chronic and/or infectious diseases, allergies to products and/or medications, regular intake of any medication and smoking habits must be reported to the doctor..
The purchase of an appropriate bra must be done in advance and it must be taken to the hospital. A button-down shirt must also be taken to the hospital, to be used on the way out. Ingestion of aspirin, ASA or any other derivative of acetyl salicylic acid should be avoided one week before surgery.

SURGERY
Surgery is always performed in a hospital to ensure patient safety. You must be admitted on the same day. Discharge is usually given 24 hours after surgery. The surgery is performed under general or epidural anesthesia and takes about three hours. After the operation, a bandage with anti-allergic tape, in the form of a bra, is made, which is changed before discharge.


Right after the surgery, you will only see an outline of the definitive shape of your breasts. They will be swollen and hyper-corrected, with the impression of slight asymmetries being common.
After the first month, the appearance begins to resemble the definitive form that is reached between the 8th and 18th months. Postoperative pain is quite rare, you may feel discomfort for a few days.
You should refrain from carrying weight, raising your elbows above your shoulders, driving a car, exercising and sunbathing for a period of three weeks after the surgery.
Intense physical exercises and heavy weights should be avoided for 2 months.
After discharge, you will be able to walk and eat normally, without being bedridden, and you can return to moderate activities 2 days after the surgery. The first return to the office should be made after 7 days, with the stitches being removed after the 15th day. Thereafter, returns are made every 15 days for the next 2 months.

THE SCARS
There are numerous techniques for mammoplasty, all leaving very similar scars, one around the areola, another in an inverted T on the lower part of the breasts.
The scar always exists, and it is up to the surgeon to make it as less noticeable as possible.
A series of intraoperative and postoperative measures contribute to this. Thus, in addition to a precise and careful surgical technique, the breasts are kept with adhesive bandages for 1 month after surgery to prevent scar enlargement. After the 1st month, dressings with silicone plates are used for sixty days.
Unfortunately, healing also depends on a number of external factors beyond our control and often beyond our control. These are hereditary factors, individual characteristics of each person's skin, smoking habit, etc.
Regardless of the final result, which is achieved about a year after the surgery, the scars will thicken and redden in the first few months, lightening and becoming less noticeable over time.

Important Issues

DOES AESTHETIC BREAST SURGERY LEAVE SCARS?

Yes, but placed in such a way that they are aesthetically acceptable and made in such a way that they are as imperceptible as possible.

WHERE ARE THE SCARS LOCATED?

Depending on the technique used, we may have variations in terms of scars. There are usually two scars located in the shape of an inverted "T", on the lower part of the breast. The one located around the areola is quite disguised by the condition of color transition between the areola and normal skin. From the first postoperative days, a very “generous” neckline can be used, as the scars are hidden. Over time, the scars become quite disguised, even reaching almost invisibility in certain cases.

I HEAR THAT SOME PATIENTS GET VERY VISIBLE SCARS. WHY DOES IT HAPPEN?

Certain patients are prone to hypertrophic scarring or keloids. This tendency, however, can be predicted, to a certain extent, during the initial consultation, when we ask a series of questions about his previous clinical life, as well as family characteristics, which help a lot in terms of the prognosis of scars. Light-skinned people rarely tend to experience this hypertrophic scarring complication.

IS THERE A CORRECTION FOR HYPERTROPHIC SCARS?

Several clinical and surgical resources allow us to improve unsightly scars at the right time. However, the “mediate period” of normal healing (from the 30th day to the 12th month) should not be confused with a scar complication.
Any doubts about your evolution should be clarified with us and never with your friends who, like you, “are also apprehensive about the final result”.

HOW WILL MY NEW BREASTS LOOK IN RELATION TO SIZE AND CONSISTENCY?

The breasts can have their volume reduced through surgery; in addition, its consistency and shape are also improved with an intervention. So, for cases of volume reduction and lifting of its position, we can opt for several volumes, within the possibilities that the original breast allows us to plan, without compromising it in the future. Here, as in the case of increased volume, the proportions between the volume of the new breast and the size of the patient's chest must be balanced in order to obtain greater aesthetic harmony. On this occasion, the sagging and shape of the original breast are corrected; however, “the new breasts” go through several evolutionary periods:

A. Immediate Period: Goes up to the 30th day. In this period, although the breasts present a much improved appearance, their shape is still below the planned result, because, in order to reach the definitive shape, we must leave “small defects” apparent at the beginning (inevitable in all cases), that disappear over time.
Remember this observation: Generally, no breast is “perfect” in the immediate postoperative period. Some small “defects” are planned to achieve the most exquisite shapes in the final (late) result.

B. Mediate Period: From the 30th day to the 8th month. In this period, the breast begins to present an evolution that tends to the definitive form, which will occur after the 8th month. Characteristics of this period are certain insensitivities of the nipple, in addition to a greater or lesser degree of “swelling” of the breasts; moreover, its form is far from definitive. Despite the euphoria of most patients, during this period we usually tell them that their results will be even better, as this will be the characteristic of the 3rd period (late).

C. Late Period: From the 8th to the 18th month. It is the period in which the breast reaches its definitive appearance (scar, shape, consistency, volume, sensitivity). It is during this period that we usually photograph the operated cases, in order to compare them with the preoperative aspect of each patient. The degree of elasticity of the skin of the breasts as well as the volume achieved is of great importance in the final result. The balance between the two varies from case to case.

HOW LONG WILL IT TAKE TO REACH THE DEFINITIVE RESULT?

Although the immediate result is very good, it is only between the 8th and 18th month that the breasts will reach their definitive shape (see previous item).
In the middle and late period any type of costume, one or two pieces, as long as the top piece is not too tight. Of course, after the scars have matured, swimsuits can be more “generous”, at your discretion. In large breast reductions, however, the horizontal scar is a little more extensive, which will determine the choice of the swimsuit that best disguises its presence.

IN THE CASE OF A NEW PREGNANCY, WILL THE RESULT REMAIN OR WILL IT BE HARMFUL?

Your gynecologist will tell you whether or not a new pregnancy is advisable. As for the result, it may be preserved, depending on the increase in breast volume during pregnancy and lactation. When dealing with very large breasts, which have been markedly reduced, lactation may be impaired. In cases of small and medium reductions, lactation can be preserved.

IS THE POST-OPERATIVE BREAST SURGERY PAINFUL?

Not. This postoperative period is painless, as long as you obey the doctor's instructions, especially with regard to moving your arms in the first few days.

IS THERE A DANGER IN THIS OPERATION?

Rarely, breast plastic surgery suffers serious complications. This is due to the fact that each patient is properly prepared, in addition to considering the convenience of associating this surgery, simultaneously with others. The danger is no greater or less than traveling by plane, automobile, or crossing a public road.

WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL PERIOD?

From 12 to 24 hours.

ARE CURATIVES USED?

Yup. Dressings and modelers, specially adapted to each type of breast. Dressings are changed periodically.

WHEN ARE THE POINTS REMOVED?

Although the vast majority of stitches are internal and there is no need to remove them, there are some external stitches that will be removed from the 15th day onwards.

WHEN WILL I TAKE A FULL BATH?

From the first day the scars can already be wet.

WHEN CAN I RETURN TO MY GYMNASTICS?

Usually after 45 days.

FROM WHAT AGE IS IT INDICATED?

There is no defined age and the indication will depend, in addition to the characteristics of the breasts, on the emotional maturity of the patient.
In general, it is recommended from the age of 15.

WHAT DISORDERS CAN BE CAUSED BY VERY LARGE AND HEAVY BREASTS?

Large, heavy and drooping breasts can cause postural problems, back pain, cervical and thoracic spine deformities and mastalgia (breast pain). In addition, there is usually significant emotional damage to the patient.

DO RESULTS LAST FOREVER?

There are several factors that will determine the duration of the result (severity, breast weight, patient weight variation, pregnancy, skin quality, hormonal effects…). Thus, sometimes, touch-ups or new surgeries may be necessary within a few years.

WHEN CAN I EXPOSE MYSELF IN THE SUN?

As long as the scars of the breasts are protected with a bikini or a paper tape, there is no harm in sunbathing from the 1st month onwards.
