Botox and Facial Fillers

Botulinum Toxin
The applications of botulinum toxin, commonly known as Botox®, for aesthetic purposes, used for more than 10 years, was a real revolution in the aesthetic treatments of facial wrinkles, being the most used aesthetic procedure in the United States, as it is extremely efficient, fast application and excellent results. The muscles of our face contract and form wrinkles. In this way, this excess muscle contraction triggers the well-known crow's feet, in the eye region, in addition to expression wrinkles that appear on the forehead and, still, creases, which can appear between the eyebrows, around the mouth and on the forehead. neck. These undesirable marks are accentuated over time.

Botulinum toxin has excellent application when the objective is to smooth expression wrinkles, which are formed by repeated muscle contraction. Recently, the food and drug administration (fda), a North American agency that regulates the use of medicines and food, approved the use of the substance for aesthetic purposes. Botulinum toxin is obtained from the bacterium Clostridium botulinum.

It is manufactured in a laboratory, where it undergoes a purification and sterilization process. Its trade names are Botox® and Dysport. The toxin can be used both in cases where wrinkles have already set in and in those where they have just started the process. In both cases, Botox® (trade name) starts to act on the neuromotor plate, blocking the nerve impulses that promote contraction. This generates muscle relaxation, which smoothes the wrinkles in the area.

The effect of Botox® is the temporary paralysis of the muscles where it is applied, preventing them from contracting, avoiding the wrinkles they cause.

The application of Botox® is done in the doctor's office and must be done by doctors and consists of small injections directly into the facial muscles that we want to paralyze. The effect starts after 48 hours, with a final result after 7 days and a durability of about 6 months, which makes the need for reapplication after that period.

From the moment you change the facial expression, the application must be done by a professional experienced in this procedure, because in the same patient we can obtain different results depending on the desired effect.

Complications are minimal, related to applications in a non-recommended place, with a temporary effect and it is a very rare case of allergy, and the application can be made in the consultation itself.

Facial Filling Facial
fillings are procedures made with numerous materials, synthetic or not, that are injected subcutaneously, below the skin, in order to fill the depression caused by the wrinkle or scar, or to increase the volume of the region, such as on the lips or nose.

Among the most common substances we have fat, hyaluronic acid (one of the well-known brands is Restylane) and PPMA (called Methacrylate or Bioplasty). Each of these substances has its indication that depends on whether the filling is more superficial or deeper, the amount used, and whether we want a temporary or permanent effect.

The advantage of temporary substances is that you can observe their effect without making it a definitive result.

The application is made in the doctor's office, by a doctor, through small injections, in a relatively quick procedure, and local anesthesia may be used in some cases. The spot tends to get a little swollen for a few days.

Substances used today rarely cause allergy or any other reaction, and can be applied at the time of consultation.
