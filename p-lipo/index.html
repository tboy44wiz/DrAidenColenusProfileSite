Liposuction and Liposculpture

It is the removal of localized fat from various areas of the body with the objective of improving body contour. It is one of the most widespread and performed procedures in plastic surgery.

Liposuction is a simple and safe surgical technique that requires a qualified professional, careful preoperative evaluation and specific instruments. In the last twenty years, it has been perfected using delicate and functional cannulas and advanced methods. Surgery presents excellent results when well indicated and when the patient is well informed about all the care and auxiliary procedures that can help him in his postoperative recovery. Your definitive results are noticed after four to six months. Indicated to correct small and large abdominal and body contour deformities, it should be performed in people with close to normal weight, with localized fat and with good skin elasticity. It is not a weight loss method. The purpose of the surgery is to improve body contour and not reduce weight

A few years ago there was a new evolution with the invention of vibroliposuction, which consists of the use of a device that, through vibration, destroys fat and aspiration at the same time, making the technique easier to perform. procedure becomes less traumatic, resulting in less bleeding, less edema and less irregularities in the postoperative period.

The volume to be aspirated in liposuction must not exceed 5% of body weight. A person weighing 70 kg, for example, is allowed to aspirate a maximum of around 3000g and 3500g.

The areas to be treated are demarcated with the patient standing so that an exact idea of ​​the area to be liposuction can be obtained. During the procedure, volume measurements and working time are used in each area in order to obtain a uniform work.

LIPOSCULTURE
Consists of removing fat from one place on the body and placing it in another place.
The fat that is removed during the procedure can be used to fill depressions, or areas that need projection: buttocks, thighs and legs, as well as on the face and hands.

Fat grafting is a procedure increasingly studied by plastic surgeons. Even on the face, where it had been almost abandoned due to the early absorption of fat, it currently receives a boost in its use, as the fat is injected into muscular and deep structures where its integration is much more effective. The preparation of fat has also been deserving of special care, since the fat cell is quite fragile and requires atraumatic handling.

Anesthesia
For minor procedures, local anesthesia with sedation is used, but when there are several areas to be treated, the option falls to epidural anesthesia. Anesthetic techniques have also evolved a lot and the monitoring devices available today are extremely efficient. Therefore, for healthy patients, liposuction, in general, poses no risks. To undergo liposuction, a careful preoperative evaluation is essential, such as clinical and complementary exams, as well as a complete clinical history of the patient and cardiovascular exam.

Procedure
On the face, for example, liposculpture has numerous indications, from sculpting a round face, due to deposition of fat on the cheeks, to uncertainties from loss of jaw angle to the treatment of double chin. Depending on the patient's age and skin quality, liposuction can be a single procedure or complementary to a full face plastic surgery. In facelift surgery, a liposculpture cannula is part of the operating table. A loss of mandibular contour can be combined with liposculpture and filling with fat.

In the nasolabial folds, strips of fat can be introduced which will fill, with the patient's own material, an area that is sometimes difficult to treat.

The erasure of the cheekbones can also be designed with fat grafting to obtain a young face with a triangular shape.

In the lips that present very intense muscular movement, the fat undergoes a greater reabsorption. However, to achieve a good result, three sessions can be programmed with an interval of three months. In the double chin, liposuction can be indicated, alone or combined with a facelift. Immobilization of the areas is important right after the intervention. During the first week, the patient is referred to physiotherapy to perform lymphatic drainage, or the application of ultrasound and other auxiliary methods. In women who undergo breast reduction and who have fat in the body of the breast, or in the axillary extension, liposuction can be associated to reduce the size of the scar.

LIPOSUCTION IN MEN
Men are increasingly concerned with their body and face contour. The treatment of double chin (double chin), waist (tires) and abdomen are the most common procedures in males. Sometimes the breasts can also be suctioned, improving the appearance of the chest.

Buttocks
Before the advent of liposuction, this fatty tissue deposition was difficult to treat. With liposuction, women with deformities in these areas have gained an arsenal of resources that provide body modeling with minimal scarring.

Knees
The knees present frequent localized fat on the inner surface. When isolated, they can be treated with local anesthesia or associated with a larger procedure.

ARM LIPOSUCTION
It is not always possible to indicate the method for this area of ​​the body, but when the arm has more localized fat and flaccidity is not important, conventional liposuction is indicated.

LIPOSUCTION COMBINED WITH ABDOMINAL PLASTIC
With the development of the technique and the anatomical knowledge of vascularization of the abdominal wall, it is now possible to perform surgery combined with liposuction. This possibility expanded the indication of these procedures. The treatment of flaccid abdominal muscles can be treated concomitantly with liposuction of the abdominal wall and waist, giving a better result.

PLANNING THE SURGERY
At the initial consultation the surgeon will assess your health, the skin and determine where the fat is located. The surgeon will explain the body contouring method that is right for you. Be upfront with the surgeon about your expectations. In this way, he will be equally frank with you.

Important Issues

THE BEST CANDIDATES FOR SURGERY

To be a good candidate for liposuction you must have realistic expectations about what the surgery can do for you. It is important to understand that liposuction can improve your appearance and self-esteem, but it will not necessarily change your appearance to the point where you achieve your ideal of beauty or cause other people to treat you differently. Before deciding to have the surgery, think carefully about your expectations and discuss them with the surgeon.
The best candidates for liposuction are people of normal weight, firm and elastic skin who have localized excess fat. You must be physically healthy, psychologically stable, and realistic with your own expectations. Their age is not a major consideration, however older patients tend to have decreased skin elasticity and therefore should not have the same results as the younger patient with elastic skin.

HOW IS LIPOSUCTION PERFORMED?

A saline solution with adrenaline is injected into the fat to be aspirated through small incisions, with the function of facilitating the removal of fat and reducing bleeding. Then the fat is removed through a thin hollow tube, called a cannula, which is placed inside the fat through small incisions in the skin, close to the places to be suctioned. The cannula is then connected to a vibro liposuction device and a vacuum aspirator that will aspirate the fat.

HOW CAN I PREPARE FOR SURGERY?

Aspirin and some other anti-inflammatories can increase bleeding, so it should be avoided for a period before surgery.
Depending on the extent of the surgery and the type of anesthesia, you will need someone to drive you home. Do not use laxative medication preoperatively.

HOW WILL I BE AND FEEL INITIALLY?

The day after the surgery, you should start to move in moderation in order to promote blood circulation. For a few days, exercises should be few and always light. You may feel some discomfort in the operated area, which is normal and controlled with painkillers.
You should also avoid aspirin and some anti-inflammatories. There will be intense edema (swelling) in the operated region in addition to ecchymosis, which are absolutely expected. The swelling will start to disappear around 30 DAYS and the ecchymosis may last for about 15 DAYS. There may be anesthesia (damping) of certain regions, which will take a few weeks to start returning.

WHAT MEDICATIONS SHOULD I USE?

An antibiotic, a pain reliever and a gel for the disappearance of bruises

WHEN WILL I BE ABLE TO RETURN TO MY NORMAL ACTIVITIES?

You can return to work in a few days and most normal activities in one to two weeks. Heavy physical activities should wait to start around 4 weeks. Recovery time will depend on the type of surgery performed and the patient.

WHAT ABOUT RESULTS?

Liposuction well indicated will bring the patient extremely satisfactory results.
Factors such as fluid retention after surgery or prolonged edema may delay the appearance of the new body contour. The healing process is gradual, so you should wait to have the final result of the surgery. The final result of the surgery happens around the 3rd month after the surgery. Eventually, some retouching may be necessary to improve the result in some liposuction area, and when this occurs, the procedure consists of correcting the affected area under local anesthesia, but always in hospital.

HOW LONG WILL THE RESULTS LAST?

The results of liposuction are permanent as long as you maintain the weight. Even if you gain weight, the tendency is for the fat to accumulate more evenly than before the surgery. Postoperative nutritional monitoring with an endocrinologist is recommended in order to maintain the results, especially for patients who are more likely to gain weight.

FOLLOW-UP AFTER SURGERY

The return after the surgery takes place after seven days after the operation, when the stitches are removed and the patient will be referred to a physical therapist who will perform lymphatic drainage, ultrasound, etc., essential for the success of the postoperative period. The compressive mesh will be used for thirty days. The patient makes monthly visits to the office until the sixth postoperative month.
